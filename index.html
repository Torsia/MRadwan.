Torque Cause Torsion

Torque applied to a shaft which is fixed from an end and free from another, cause torsion stress inside the shaft.

Torsion Cause Twist

Shafts twists in the presence of torsion. The Angle of Twist is a measure for twist happened due to torque.

Torsion Cause Shear stress

The presence of torsion generates a distributed shear stress all over the cross-sectional area.

Experimental Assumptions

  • Material is uniform throughout the shaft.
  • Diameter of the cylinder stays the same after deformation.
  • Cross-section of the shaft, which are plane before twist remain plane after twist.
  • The twist along the shaft is uniform.
